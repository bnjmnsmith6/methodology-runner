# Materials Research: Reusable Coffee Cup Handle

## Scope
Material candidates for a universal reusable handle that attaches to standard hot paper coffee cups. Handle must withstand coffee service temperatures (160–180°F), survive 100+ thermal use cycles, and meet FDA food contact surface regulations.

---

## Candidate Material Categories

### 1. Food-Grade Silicones

#### 1.1 Platinum-Cured Silicone (VMQ/PVMQ)
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | Polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) cross-linked with platinum catalyst |
| Continuous service temp | -65°F to 450°F (–54°C to 232°C) |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Excellent — no degradation |
| FDA status | 21 CFR §177.2600 (rubber articles for repeated use) |
| Dishwasher safe | Yes — tolerates 140–160°F wash cycles |
| Leach risk | Very low — platinum cure leaves no by-products |
| Cycle rating (estimated) | 500+ thermal cycles without property loss |
| Safety notes | Must be 100% platinum-cured; avoid peroxide-cured grades for food contact |

#### 1.2 Peroxide-Cured Silicone
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | PDMS cross-linked with peroxide initiators |
| Continuous service temp | -65°F to 392°F (–54°C to 200°C) |
| FDA status | Conditionally listed under 21 CFR §177.2600; residual peroxide by-products require post-cure verification |
| Leach risk | Moderate — residual peroxides if post-cure not performed |
| Cycle rating (estimated) | 300+ cycles |
| Safety notes | Requires documented post-cure step; less preferred than platinum-cured for this application |

### 2. Engineering Thermoplastics

#### 2.1 PEEK (Polyether ether ketone)
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | Semi-crystalline aromatic polymer (repeating ether-ketone-ether-ketone-ketone units) |
| Continuous service temp | Up to 480°F (250°C) |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Excellent — far below glass transition temperature (289°F / 143°C) |
| FDA status | 21 CFR §177.2415 (polyarylether ketone resins) — food contact approved |
| Dishwasher safe | Yes |
| Leach risk | Very low — highly chemically inert |
| Cycle rating (estimated) | 1,000+ cycles |
| Safety notes | Higher material cost; provides rigid structural component; excellent fatigue resistance |

#### 2.2 PEI (Polyetherimide / Ultem)
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | Amorphous thermoplastic with imide and ether linkages |
| Continuous service temp | Up to 338°F (170°C) |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Excellent — Tg is 419°F (215°C) |
| FDA status | 21 CFR §177.1630 (polyimide resins) — food contact approved with restrictions |
| Dishwasher safe | Yes |
| Leach risk | Low |
| Cycle rating (estimated) | 500+ cycles |
| Safety notes | Amber/translucent color may affect aesthetics; verify specific grade compliance with CFR section |

#### 2.3 Polypropylene (PP) — Food Grade
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | Isotactic polypropylene (–CH₂–CH(CH₃)–)ₙ |
| Continuous service temp | Up to 266°F (130°C) short-term; 212°F (100°C) continuous |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Marginal — near upper continuous service limit; risk of creep under load |
| FDA status | 21 CFR §177.1520 (olefin polymers) — broadly approved |
| Dishwasher safe | Yes (top rack) |
| Leach risk | Very low when food-grade grade selected |
| Cycle rating (estimated) | 100–200 cycles before potential deformation |
| Safety notes | Not recommended as primary material due to marginal thermal margin; acceptable as grip insert only |

#### 2.4 High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) — Food Grade
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | Linear polyethylene (–CH₂–CH₂–)ₙ, high density |
| Continuous service temp | Up to 230°F (110°C) |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Marginal — potential softening at upper coffee temperatures |
| FDA status | 21 CFR §177.1520 — approved |
| Dishwasher safe | Conditional — may warp at high temps |
| Safety notes | Not recommended for structural load-bearing at 180°F; excluded from primary candidates |

### 3. Metal Alloys

#### 3.1 Food-Grade Stainless Steel (304 / 316)
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | 304: Fe/Cr18/Ni8; 316: Fe/Cr16/Ni10/Mo2 (Mo improves corrosion resistance) |
| Continuous service temp | Up to 1,650°F (900°C) — essentially unlimited for this use |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Excellent |
| FDA status | Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) for food contact; compliant with 21 CFR §170.39 principles; FDA does not specifically list metals but CFSAN guidance and NSF/ANSI 51 cover food equipment metals |
| Dishwasher safe | Yes — 316 preferred for chloride environments |
| Leach risk | Very low — passive oxide layer; 316 superior to 304 in acidic/saline conditions |
| Cycle rating (estimated) | 10,000+ cycles — effectively unlimited |
| Safety notes | Conducts heat; requires insulating grip layer (silicone overmold or sleeve) to prevent burns; heavier than polymer alternatives |

#### 3.2 Aluminum Alloy (6061-T6 anodized)
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical composition | Al/Mg1/Si0.6 |
| Continuous service temp | Up to 300°F (149°C) |
| Heat resistance at 180°F | Excellent |
| FDA status | Aluminum is generally recognized as safe; anodize layer must meet FDA food contact guidance; bare aluminum may react with acidic beverages |
| Leach risk | Low with hard-anodize; moderate with bare aluminum in acidic conditions |
| Safety notes | Lighter than steel; requires hard-anodize coating for food contact; thermal conductivity requires insulating grip; less preferred than stainless for food contact certainty |

## Preliminary Shortlist (advancing to compliance matrix)
1. **Platinum-cured food-grade silicone** — primary grip/insulation material
2. **PEEK** — primary structural frame material
3. **Stainless Steel 316** — alternative structural material
4. **PEI (Ultem)** — backup structural material

PP and HDPE excluded from primary candidates due to marginal thermal safety margins at continuous 180°F exposure.
