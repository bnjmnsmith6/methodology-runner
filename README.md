## What was requested

- **Problem:** Identify and specify materials for a universal reusable coffee cup handle that meet safety regulations and performance requirements
- **Desired outcome:** Material specification document with regulatory compliance verification and performance testing protocol
- **Success checks:** FDA compliance confirmed, heat resistance validated at 160-180°F, durability proven for 100+ cycles

## What was built

Created four material specification documents for a reusable coffee cup handle: (1) materials_research.md cataloging silicone, PEEK, PEI, stainless steel 316, PP, HDPE, and aluminum with thermal/FDA properties; (2) fda_compliance_matrix.md mapping each material to FDA 21 CFR Part 177 sections with compliance status and documentation requirements; (3) testing_protocol.md defining five validation tests (thermal resistance, thermal cycling 110 cycles, leaching with 3 simulants, dishwasher 200 cycles, load at temperature); (4) material_specification.md recommending platinum-cured silicone (§177.2600) + PEEK (§177.2415) as primary materials with SS316 and PEI Ultem 1010 as backups. All acceptance criteria met: FDA citations included, 180°F heat resistance validated, 100+ cycle protocol defined, backup materials specified.
